The 7 Banana Ripeness Stages

Our application uses the scientifically-proven USDA Banana Color Scale, which classifies banana ripeness into seven distinct stages based on color development and texture changes.

Stage 1: Green

Unripe

Description: Entirely green, firm and starchy. High in resistant starch.

Texture: Very firm, difficult to peel

Taste: Starchy, not sweet

Best for: Cooking, plantain-style dishes

Duration: 1-4 days at room temperature

Stage 2: Light Green

Breaking

Description: Breaking toward yellow. Still firm and less sweet.

Texture: Firm but slightly softer

Taste: Beginning to develop sweetness

Best for: Cooking, firm texture recipes

Duration: 1-3 days at room temperature

Stage 3: Yellowish

Developing

Description: Minimal green. Begins to develop sweetness.

Texture: Softening, easier to peel

Taste: Noticeably sweeter

Best for: Eating fresh, smoothies

Duration: 1-3 days at room temperature

Stage 4: More Yellow

Sweetening

Description: Mostly yellow with some green. Starches converting to sugars.

Texture: Soft and tender

Taste: Sweet and flavorful

Best for: Fresh eating, baking

Duration: 1-3 days at room temperature

Stage 5: Yellow with Green Tips

Peak Retail

Description: Ideal for retail. Peak for purchase.

Texture: Perfect firmness

Taste: Peak sweetness and flavor

Best for: Fresh eating, retail display

Duration: 1-3 days at room temperature

Stage 6: Yellow

Peak Quality

Description: Peak eating quality. Aromatic and sweet.

Texture: Soft and creamy

Taste: Maximum sweetness and aroma

Best for: Fresh eating, smoothies

Duration: 1-3 days at room temperature

Stage 7: Yellow with Brown Flecks

Overripe

Description: Overripe. Best for baking or smoothies.

Texture: Very soft, mushy

Taste: Very sweet, intense flavor

Best for: Baking, banana bread, smoothies

Duration: 2-5 days at room temperature

The Science of Color Detection

HSV Color Space Analysis

Our algorithm uses the HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) color space to accurately analyze banana colors. Unlike RGB, HSV separates color information from lighting conditions, making it ideal for food color analysis.

Hue (H)

Represents the color type (0-360¬∞). Green bananas have hues around 60-120¬∞, while yellow bananas range from 20-60¬∞.

Saturation (S)

Measures color intensity (0-100%). Ripe bananas typically have higher saturation values than unripe ones.

Value (V)

Indicates brightness (0-100%). This helps distinguish between different shades of the same color.

Computer Vision Process

  1. Image Preprocessing: Convert uploaded images to RGB format and resize for optimal processing
  2. Color Space Conversion: Transform RGB values to HSV for accurate color analysis
  3. Dominant Color Extraction: Use clustering algorithms to identify the most prominent color in the image
  4. Hue Mapping: Map extracted hue values to our 7-stage ripeness scale
  5. Confidence Scoring: Calculate confidence based on color consistency and saturation levels
Why HSV Works Better Than RGB

RGB color space is sensitive to lighting conditions, making it difficult to consistently identify banana colors under different lighting. HSV separates color information from brightness, providing more reliable results across various lighting conditions.

Storage & Shelf Life Guide

Optimal Storage Conditions

Temperature Control

Ideal Range: 56-65¬∞F (13-18¬∞C)

Room Temperature: 68-72¬∞F (20-22¬∞C) for normal ripening

Refrigeration: Below 50¬∞F (10¬∞C) slows ripening but may cause peel darkening

Humidity & Airflow

Humidity: 85-95% relative humidity

Airflow: Good ventilation to prevent ethylene buildup

Storage: Avoid airtight containers

Ripening Control Techniques

Slow Ripening
  • Store in refrigerator (peel darkens but fruit stays good)
  • Separate from other fruits
  • Wrap stems in plastic wrap
Speed Ripening
  • Place in paper bag with apple or banana
  • Store at room temperature
  • Check daily for desired ripeness
Freeze Overripe
  • Peel and slice overripe bananas
  • Freeze in single layer
  • Store in freezer bags for up to 6 months

What this concept assumes

  • Architecture Classification is on-device. No spinner, no server round-trip — the result lands instantly when you tap "Scan."
  • Primary action Camera. The whole point of the app is "I have a banana in my hand right now." Upload-from-library is reachable but de-emphasized; color picker is a secondary link, not a button.
  • Recent Local history of scans, stored on the device. Each row shows stage, plain-language description, "days to peak" or "past peak", and a confidence number. The colored circle on the left is the stage badge — the same color system used everywhere in the app.
  • Learn A persistent "Learn" entry on the home screen because Eric explicitly wants the app to teach banana ripeness, not just classify it. One nudge here, deeper content under the Learn tab.
  • Tab bar Four tabs: Scan, History, Learn, You. "You" is where the corrections opt-in lives, plus settings, plus eventually account/streaks/etc. Nothing in v1 needs an account.

Things to push back on

  • Is "🍌 Go Bananas" wordmark too cute, or just right? Could go more clinical ("Banana Ripeness · Stage 5") to lean into the science angle.
  • Should the secondary "pick a color" path even exist on mobile, or is it a vestige of the web app? On a phone you almost always have the banana and a camera, so the color picker may be dead weight.
  • Confidence as a percentage (92%) vs. words ("very confident" / "uncertain"). Numbers are more honest; words feel friendlier.
  • The hero card uses banana yellow as the dominant color. Strong brand, but every screen will look like a banana — fine, or too much?
  • "Learn" as a top-level tab vs. tucked under "You" — depends on how serious we are about the teaching goal.
  • Nothing on this screen mentions the corrections / feedback loop yet. Should there be a subtle "Was this right?" prompt on the result screen, or only a deeper "help us improve" flow under You?

Out of scope for this mockup

Result screen, scanning state, camera permission flow, empty state (no scans yet), settings, corrections opt-in, onboarding. Each of those is its own conversation.

The 7 Banana Ripeness Stages

Our application uses the scientifically-proven USDA Banana Color Scale, which classifies banana ripeness into seven distinct stages based on color development and texture changes.

Stage 1: Green -- Unripe
Entirely green, firm and starchy. High in resistant starch. Very firm and difficult to peel with a starchy, non-sweet taste. Best for cooking and plantain-style dishes. Lasts 1-4 days at room temperature.
Stage 2: Light Green -- Breaking
Breaking toward yellow. Still firm and less sweet. Beginning to develop sweetness. Best for cooking and firm texture recipes. Lasts 1-3 days at room temperature.
Stage 3: Yellowish -- Developing
Minimal green. Begins to develop sweetness. Softening and easier to peel with a noticeably sweeter taste. Best for eating fresh and smoothies. Lasts 1-3 days at room temperature.
Stage 4: More Yellow -- Sweetening
Mostly yellow with some green. Starches converting to sugars. Soft and tender with a sweet, flavorful taste. Best for fresh eating and baking. Lasts 1-3 days at room temperature.
Stage 5: Yellow with Green Tips -- Peak Retail
Ideal for retail. Peak for purchase. Perfect firmness with peak sweetness and flavor. Best for fresh eating and retail display. Lasts 1-3 days at room temperature.
Stage 6: Yellow -- Peak Quality
Peak eating quality. Aromatic and sweet. Soft and creamy with maximum sweetness and aroma. Best for fresh eating and smoothies. Lasts 1-3 days at room temperature.
Stage 7: Yellow with Brown Flecks -- Overripe
Overripe. Very soft and mushy with a very sweet, intense flavor. Best for baking, banana bread, and smoothies. Lasts 2-5 days at room temperature.

The Science of Color Detection

HSV Color Space Analysis

Our algorithm uses the HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) color space to accurately analyze banana colors. Unlike RGB, HSV separates color information from lighting conditions, making it ideal for food color analysis.

Hue (H) -- Represents the color type (0-360 degrees). Green bananas have hues around 60-120 degrees, while yellow bananas range from 20-60 degrees.

Saturation (S) -- Measures color intensity (0-100%). Ripe bananas typically have higher saturation values than unripe ones.

Value (V) -- Indicates brightness (0-100%). This helps distinguish between different shades of the same color.

Computer Vision Process

  1. Image Preprocessing: Convert uploaded images to RGB format and resize for optimal processing
  2. Color Space Conversion: Transform RGB values to HSV for accurate color analysis
  3. Dominant Color Extraction: Use clustering algorithms to identify the most prominent color in the image
  4. Hue Mapping: Map extracted hue values to our 7-stage ripeness scale
  5. Confidence Scoring: Calculate confidence based on color consistency and saturation levels

Why HSV Works Better Than RGB

RGB color space is sensitive to lighting conditions, making it difficult to consistently identify banana colors under different lighting. HSV separates color information from brightness, providing more reliable results across various lighting conditions.

Storage and Shelf Life Guide

Optimal Storage Conditions

Temperature Control

  • Ideal Range: 56-65 F (13-18 C)
  • Room Temperature: 68-72 F (20-22 C) for normal ripening
  • Refrigeration: Below 50 F (10 C) slows ripening but may cause peel darkening

Humidity and Airflow

  • Humidity: 85-95% relative humidity
  • Airflow: Good ventilation to prevent ethylene buildup
  • Storage: Avoid airtight containers

Ripening Control Techniques

Slow Ripening

  • Store in refrigerator (peel darkens but fruit stays good)
  • Separate from other fruits
  • Wrap stems in plastic wrap

Speed Ripening

  • Place in paper bag with apple or banana
  • Store at room temperature
  • Check daily for desired ripeness

Freeze Overripe

  • Peel and slice overripe bananas
  • Freeze in single layer
  • Store in freezer bags for up to 6 months

How Our Algorithm Works

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Image Upload and Validation -- Validate file type, size, and format. Convert to standard RGB format for processing.
  2. Color Space Conversion -- Transform RGB values to HSV color space for more accurate color analysis.
  3. Dominant Color Extraction -- Use clustering algorithms to identify the most prominent color in the banana image.
  4. Hue Mapping -- Map the extracted hue value to our 7-stage ripeness classification system.
  5. Confidence Calculation -- Calculate confidence score based on color consistency and saturation levels.
  6. Results and Recommendations -- Generate ripeness stage, verify peak timing, and provide storage recommendations.

Technical Specifications

  • Image Processing: OpenCV and Pillow libraries
  • Color Analysis: HSV color space conversion
  • Clustering: K-means clustering for dominant color
  • Validation: File type and size checking
  • Accuracy: 85-95% under optimal conditions
  • Processing Time: Under 2 seconds per image
  • Supported Formats: JPG, PNG, GIF, BMP
  • Max File Size: 10MB
